Section 01 · Investor Charter

Investor Charter for Research Analysts

Vision

Informed, protected investors

To provide investors with high-quality, independent equity research in a transparent, ethical, and disclosure-first manner, enabling informed investment decisions within a robust regulatory framework.

Mission

Disciplined, disclosure-led research

To deliver research products grounded in primary data and documented methodology, while upholding the highest standards of compliance, integrity, and investor protection prescribed by SEBI.

Services provided to investors

  • ✓Independent equity research reports on listed Indian companies.
  • ✓Sector and thematic deep-dives.
  • ✓Periodic update notes on covered names.
  • ✓Methodology memos explaining the research process.

Rights of investors

  • ✓Right to fair, honest, and unbiased research.
  • ✓Right to full disclosure of conflicts of interest and holdings.
  • ✓Right to a clear, written agreement and fee schedule.
  • ✓Right to confidentiality of personal information.
  • ✓Right to grievance redressal under SEBI's framework.
  • ✓Right to discontinue services on appropriate notice.

Responsibilities of investors

  • ✓Provide accurate KYC and risk-profile information.
  • ✓Read research notes carefully, including the disclosure pack.
  • ✓Make investment decisions based on personal risk appetite.
  • ✓Pay fees only through the registered banking channel.
  • ✓Raise grievances promptly through the prescribed channel.
  • ✓Avoid acting on tips from unregulated sources.
Do's
  • ✓Verify SEBI registration on the official SEBI portal.
  • ✓Read the agreement, MITC, and fee schedule before paying.
  • ✓Pay fees only into the registered RA bank account.
  • ✓Always check the disclosure pack accompanying each note.
  • ✓Use SCORES / SmartODR if a grievance is unresolved.
Don'ts
  • ×Do not act on unsolicited "tips" or guaranteed-return offers.
  • ×Do not share login credentials or OTPs with anyone.
  • ×Do not pay fees in cash or to third-party accounts.
  • ×Do not invest based on social-media chatter alone.
  • ×Do not ignore risk disclosures and assumptions.
Section 02 · Grievance Redressal

Four levels of escalation, in this order.

If something goes wrong, here is how to raise it. Each level has a defined timeline. Skip a level and SEBI may refer the matter back.

  1. 1

    Reach out to the Research Analyst

    Email kishlay121@gmail.com or call +91 91100 52543. Acknowledgement within 2 working days; resolution targeted within 21 calendar days.

    Step 1 · Direct
  2. 2

    Escalate to SEBI SCORES

    If unresolved, lodge a complaint on the SEBI Complaints Redress System at scores.sebi.gov.in. SEBI helpline: 1800 22 7575 / 1800 266 7575 (toll-free).

    Step 2 · Regulator portal
  3. 3

    Online Dispute Resolution, SmartODR

    For monetary disputes, escalate to the SmartODR portal at smartodr.in. Conciliation and arbitration are conducted online by SEBI-recognised ODR institutions.

    Step 3 · ODR
  4. 4

    Approach SEBI / appropriate forum

    For matters outside SCORES / SmartODR, investors may approach SEBI directly or the appropriate civil / consumer forum as per applicable law.

    Step 4 · Final recourse

Designated officer

For this single-analyst practice, the Principal Officer, Compliance Officer and Grievance Officer is Kishlay Kumar, reachable at kishlay121@gmail.com / +91 91100 52543.

Section 04 · Disclosures

Standard disclosures & disclaimer

Standard disclosures

  • ✓Kishlay Kumar is registered with SEBI as a Research Analyst (Reg. No. INH000022826).
  • ✓The analyst is suitably qualified and has obtained the required NISM certifications under SEBI norms.
  • ✓No part of the analyst's compensation is linked to any specific recommendation or view.
  • ✓The analyst does not engage in market-making activity.
  • ✓Specific holdings, conflicts, and prior interests (if any) are disclosed alongside each individual research note.
  • ✓The analyst has not received any compensation for the publication of any research note from the subject company in the preceding 12 months.

Standard disclaimer

Investments in securities markets are subject to market risks. Read all the related documents carefully before investing. Registration granted by SEBI and certification from NISM in no way guarantee performance of the intermediary or provide any assurance of returns to investors.

The content provided through this site and through any research note is for informational purposes only and does not constitute an offer, solicitation, or personalised investment advice. Recipients should consider their own financial position, risk appetite, and consult an appropriately qualified advisor before making any investment decision.

Standard warning

Investment in securities market is subject to market risks. Read all the related documents carefully before investing.

Caution to investors

Beware of fraudulent schemes promising guaranteed or assured returns. Pay only into the registered RA bank account. Never share login credentials, OTPs, or signed cheques. Verify SEBI registration on sebi.gov.in before engaging any intermediary.

Section 05 · KYC Notice

KYC notice for investors

KYC is a one-time exercise while dealing in securities markets. Once KYC is done through a SEBI-registered intermediary (Broker, DP, Mutual Fund, etc.), you need not undergo the same process again when you approach another intermediary.

  • ✓KYC is mandatory before any paid research engagement.
  • ✓Provide accurate identity, address, and risk-profile information.
  • ✓Receipts and tax invoices are issued for every payment.
  • ✓Personal information is held in strict confidentiality.
