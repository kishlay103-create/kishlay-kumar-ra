Research catalogue

Four research formats.
No tips, no shortcuts.

Each format below is a research product, long-form, methodology-led, with a stated cadence. Fee structure is shared on request and falls within SEBI's prescribed cap for Research Analysts.

SR / 01

Equity Research Reports

Initiating-coverage and update notes on listed Indian companies. Each report includes business and industry context, a quantitative model, valuation under base/bull/bear scenarios, and a transparent risk register.

  • ✓Initiating coverage: 20-40 page note.
  • ✓Update notes published when the thesis materially changes.
  • ✓Disclosure pack with every note.
Long-form · 20-40 pp
SR / 02

Sector Deep-Dives

Quarterly studies of a sector's structure, regulation, demand drivers, and the listed names worth tracking. Useful for investors who want to allocate by industry rather than by name.

  • ✓One sector per quarter, chosen on relevance.
  • ✓Regulatory and policy backdrop included.
  • ✓Comparative scorecards across listed peers.
Quarterly
SR / 03

Result-Season Notes

Concise post-results commentary on names under coverage. Each note states what changed in the thesis, what stayed intact, and how forecasts have been revised, with full assumption notes.

  • ✓Published within the earnings cycle.
  • ✓Forecast revisions flagged explicitly.
  • ✓Linked back to the most recent full report.
Earnings cycle
SR / 04

Methodology Memos

Periodic memos on the research process itself, model templates, valuation choices, position-sizing logic, and how risk is framed in published work. Free for all readers.

  • ✓Published as the practice evolves.
  • ✓No proprietary models hidden behind paywalls.
  • ✓Open for critique and revision.
As needed
Methodology

A four-step pipeline. Documented, not improvised.

The same pipeline runs on every name. The shape of the output may change; the rigour does not.

01 / Screen

Filter the universe

Quantitative screens (quality, growth, governance) plus qualitative filters reduce hundreds of names to a working shortlist.

02 / Read

Read the primaries

Annual reports, regulatory filings, conference call transcripts, competitor disclosures, read fully, not skimmed for soundbites.

03 / Model

Model bottom-up

Forecasts, valuation, and scenarios are built line-by-line. Every key assumption is stated and stress-tested.

04 / Disclose

Publish with disclosures

Conflicts, holdings, risk register, and methodology travel with every note. Nothing important lives in a footer.

Fee structure

Within the SEBI cap, on request.

Fees for Research Analyst services are charged within the maximum permissible amount prescribed by SEBI under Regulation 15A of the SEBI (Research Analysts) Regulations, 2014, read with applicable circulars and the rates notified through the Centralised Fee Collection Mechanism (CeFCoM).

The current schedule, including any tiered options, is shared on request after a brief KYC. Fees are payable only through banking channels into the registered RA account; cash and informal payments are not accepted.

Quick reference

What you can expect

  • ✓Fee mode, Fixed (no AUM-linked or success-linked fees).
  • ✓Payment channel, Bank transfer / UPI to the registered RA account only.
  • ✓Refunds, Pro-rata, on written request, per RA service agreement.
  • ✓KYC, Mandatory before onboarding any paid client.
  • ✓Receipts, Issued for every payment, with GST as applicable.

All fees are subject to applicable taxes. The fee cap and CeFCoM mechanism are determined by SEBI and may be revised by the regulator from time to time.

Caution to investors

Pay fees only through verified banking channels into the SEBI-registered RA account, against an invoice issued by the analyst. Be wary of any third-party demanding payment in the analyst's name through other channels.
